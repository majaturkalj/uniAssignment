Online mapping tool gives FGM runaways a path to help

We are helping to trace roads and buildings making navigable maps, which allow rescuers to get help to stranded girls.

"Nearly 600 international mappers have worked to help fill in the map around a safe house for girls running away from FGM and other gender-based violence located in Mugumu in the Serengeti district, Tanzania. Their work has resulted in the rescue of several girls and one arrest. Volunteers use satellite photographs on OpenStreetMap, a free crowd-sourced mapping service, to trace roads and buildings from the images, missing from printed and other online maps, and turn them into navigable maps.
