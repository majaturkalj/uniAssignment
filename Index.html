About us

We are a group of online volunteers who help diminish the practice of Female Genital Mutilation or FGM. This awful practice is still alive and prevalent in over 20 countries. Per WHO (2008), it is estimated that between 100 and 140 million of girls and women have undergone through such a procedure. Every year, there are 3 million girls under the risk of this practice. (Source: WHO 2008)

Our online volunteers collaborate with volunteers in Tanzania and worldwide. We are a part of the Missing Maps and Crowd2Map Tanzania project. Online maps are almost non-existant, especially in rural Tanzania. Adding buildings and roads to an OpenStreetMap will help better delivery and monitoring of services, and it will improve navigation and visibility.


A comprehensive research and library can be found on the site of 28 Too Many organization. This is a registred charity undertaking research and providing knowledge and tools for people working to end FGM. (Source: 28 Too Many 2018)
